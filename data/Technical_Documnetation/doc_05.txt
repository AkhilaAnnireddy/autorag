
      # Introduction to Database Schema Documentation
      The database schema documentation is a comprehensive guide to the structure and organization of the company's database. This document is intended for database administrators, developers, and other stakeholders who need to understand the database schema. The database is designed to support the company's e-commerce platform, which was launched on February 10, 2020, and has since become a critical component of the company's operations.

      The database schema is based on a relational model, with 15 tables and 50 relationships between them. The tables are organized into five categories: customer information, order management, product catalog, inventory management, and reporting. Each table has a unique identifier, and the relationships between tables are established through foreign keys.

      ## Customer Information
      The customer information category includes three tables: customers, addresses, and contact information. The customers table has 10 columns, including customer ID, name, email, and phone number. The addresses table has 8 columns, including address ID, customer ID, street address, and city. The contact information table has 5 columns, including contact ID, customer ID, phone number, and email.

      The following table shows the structure of the customers table:
      | Column Name | Data Type | Description |
      | --- | --- | --- |
      | customer_id | int | Unique customer identifier |
      | name | varchar(50) | Customer name |
      | email | varchar(100) | Customer email |
      | phone_number | varchar(20) | Customer phone number |
      | created_at | timestamp | Timestamp when the customer was created |
      | updated_at | timestamp | Timestamp when the customer was last updated |

      In addition to the customers table, the customer information category also includes two views: customer_summary and customer_details. The customer_summary view provides a summary of customer information, including the number of orders, total spend, and average order value. The customer_details view provides detailed information about each customer, including their address and contact information.

      ## Order Management
      The order management category includes five tables: orders, order_items, payments, shipping, and order_status. The orders table has 12 columns, including order ID, customer ID, order date, and total amount. The order_items table has 8 columns, including order item ID, order ID, product ID, and quantity. The payments table has 6 columns, including payment ID, order ID, payment method, and payment date.

      The following are the key policies and procedures for order management:
      * All orders must be processed within 24 hours of receipt.
      * Orders can be cancelled or modified up to 2 hours before shipping.
      * Payment must be received before shipping.
      * Shipping is typically done within 3-5 business days.

      ## Product Catalog
      The product catalog category includes three tables: products, product_categories, and product_variations. The products table has 10 columns, including product ID, name, description, and price. The product_categories table has 5 columns, including category ID, category name, and description. The product_variations table has 8 columns, including variation ID, product ID, and variation name.

      The product catalog is updated quarterly, with new products added and old products removed. The product catalog is also subject to change based on customer feedback and market trends.

      ## Inventory Management
      The inventory management category includes two tables: inventory and stock_levels. The inventory table has 8 columns, including inventory ID, product ID, and quantity. The stock_levels table has 5 columns, including stock level ID, product ID, and stock level.

      The following are the key performance indicators (KPIs) for inventory management:
      * Inventory turnover: 5 times per year
      * Stockout rate: less than 2%
      * Overstock rate: less than 5%

      ## Reporting
      The reporting category includes two tables: sales and customer_segmentation. The sales table has 10 columns, including sales ID, order ID, and sales amount. The customer_segmentation table has 8 columns, including segmentation ID, customer ID, and segmentation name.

      The sales table is updated daily, and the customer_segmentation table is updated weekly. The reporting category is used to generate reports on sales, customer behavior, and market trends.
