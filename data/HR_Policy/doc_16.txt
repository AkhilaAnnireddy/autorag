
      # Introduction to Termination Policy
      The termination policy at NovaTech Inc. is designed to ensure that all employees are treated fairly and with respect. This policy applies to all regular full-time and part-time employees.

      ## Purpose and Scope
      The purpose of this policy is to outline the procedures and guidelines for terminating an employee's employment with NovaTech Inc. This policy covers all aspects of termination, including voluntary and involuntary termination, and applies to all employees, regardless of their position or length of service.

      # Definitions
      For the purpose of this policy, the following definitions apply:
      - **Termination**: The end of an employee's employment with NovaTech Inc.
      - **Voluntary Termination**: When an employee chooses to leave their position with NovaTech Inc.
      - **Involuntary Termination**: When NovaTech Inc. terminates an employee's employment.

      ## Termination Procedures
      All terminations must be approved by the Director of Human Resources and must follow the procedures outlined below. 
      The employee's supervisor or manager must provide written notice of termination to the Director of Human Resources at least 14 days prior to the termination date.
      The notice must include the reason for termination, the date of termination, and any other relevant details.

      # Voluntary Termination
      Employees who wish to leave their position with NovaTech Inc. must provide written notice to their supervisor or manager at least 2 weeks prior to their last day of work.
      The notice must include the date of the last day of work and any other relevant details.
      Employees who provide less than 2 weeks' notice may be eligible for rehire, but this will be determined on a case-by-case basis.

      ## Involuntary Termination
      NovaTech Inc. may terminate an employee's employment for a variety of reasons, including but not limited to:
      - Poor performance
      - Misconduct
      - Redundancy
      - Business requirements.
      The decision to terminate an employee's employment will be made by the Director of Human Resources, in consultation with the employee's supervisor or manager.
      The employee will be provided with written notice of termination, which will include the reason for termination, the date of termination, and any other relevant details.

      # Severance Package
      Employees who are terminated involuntarily may be eligible for a severance package, which will include:
      - 2 weeks' pay for every year of service, up to a maximum of 12 weeks
      - Continuation of benefits for a period of 3 months
      - Outplacement assistance.
      The severance package will be determined on a case-by-case basis and will be based on the employee's length of service and other relevant factors.

      ## Appeal Process
      Employees who are terminated and believe that the termination was unfair or unjust may appeal the decision to the CEO.
      The appeal must be made in writing and must include the reasons for the appeal and any supporting evidence.
      The CEO will review the appeal and make a decision, which will be final and binding.

      # Termination of Contract Employees
      Contract employees are not eligible for severance packages or outplacement assistance.
      Contract employees who are terminated will receive written notice of termination, which will include the reason for termination and the date of termination.

      ## Record Keeping
      All terminations will be documented and records will be kept on file for a period of 5 years.
      The records will include the reason for termination, the date of termination, and any other relevant details.

      # Employee Responsibilities
      Employees who are terminated must return all company property, including their laptop, phone, and any other company materials.
      Employees must also return their ID badge and any other company identification.

      ## Manager Responsibilities
      Managers and supervisors are responsible for ensuring that all terminations are handled in accordance with this policy.
      Managers and supervisors must also ensure that all necessary paperwork is completed and that the employee's file is updated.

      # Policy Review and Update
      This policy will be reviewed and updated annually, or as necessary.
      The policy will be communicated to all employees and will be available on the company intranet.

      As of the date of this policy, February 10, 2024, all employees are expected to adhere to the guidelines outlined above.
