Our company, GreenTech Inc., is committed to protecting the personal data of our customers, employees, and partners. We believe that transparency and accountability are essential in maintaining trust and ensuring the responsible handling of sensitive information. As of January 1, 2022, we have implemented a comprehensive data privacy policy that outlines our practices and procedures for collecting, storing, and utilizing personal data. This policy applies to all GreenTech Inc. subsidiaries, including GreenTech Europe and GreenTech Asia, and covers all aspects of our operations, from employee management to customer relations. Our data protection officer, Ms. Emma Taylor, is responsible for overseeing the implementation and enforcement of this policy, and can be reached at emma.taylor@greentech.com. The contact information for our data protection officer is also available on our website, www.greentech.com, under the 'Contact Us' section. We collect various types of personal data, including names, addresses, phone numbers, and email addresses, primarily through our website, social media platforms, and direct interactions with customers and employees. According to our data collection policy, which was last updated on June 15, 2022, we only collect data that is necessary for the purposes of conducting business, providing services, and complying with legal requirements. For instance, we use cookies on our website to track user behavior and improve our services, and we require explicit consent from users before collecting any sensitive information. Our data retention policy, which was approved by our board of directors on March 10, 2022, states that we will retain personal data for a period of five years from the date of collection, unless otherwise required by law or regulatory requirements. We have established a data breach response plan, which includes procedures for notification, containment, and mitigation, and we conduct regular security audits to ensure the integrity and confidentiality of our systems. In the event of a data breach, we will notify affected individuals within 72 hours, as required by the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), and provide them with information on the nature of the breach, the measures we are taking to address it, and any steps they can take to protect themselves. Our data protection training program, which was launched on August 1, 2022, includes regular workshops and online courses for employees, and we also provide training for our partners and vendors on data handling and protection. We have established partnerships with third-party service providers, including Amazon Web Services and Microsoft Azure, to support our data storage and processing needs, and we ensure that these providers adhere to our data protection standards and policies. As of September 1, 2022, we have implemented a data subject access request process, which allows individuals to request access to their personal data, and we respond to such requests within 30 days. We also provide individuals with the right to rectify, erase, or restrict the processing of their personal data, and we have established a complaints handling procedure to address any concerns or issues related to our data handling practices. Our data protection policy is reviewed and updated annually, or as needed, to ensure that it remains effective and compliant with changing regulatory requirements. The next review is scheduled for December 31, 2023. We are committed to upholding the highest standards of data protection and privacy, and we will continue to monitor and improve our practices to ensure the trust and confidence of our customers, employees, and partners.
