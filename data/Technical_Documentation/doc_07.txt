The CI/CD pipeline setup guide is a crucial document for any development team, as it outlines the steps necessary to automate the build, test, and deployment of software applications. At NovaTech, our development team has been using Jenkins as the primary CI/CD tool since 2018, when we first implemented it on January 10th. The decision to use Jenkins was made by our CTO, Rachel Lee, who has over 10 years of experience in DevOps. According to our internal policy, Document ID: NT-001, all new projects must have a CI/CD pipeline set up within the first two weeks of project initiation. The pipeline consists of three main stages: build, test, and deploy. In the build stage, the code is compiled and packaged into a deployable artifact. This stage takes approximately 10 minutes to complete, depending on the complexity of the code. Our development team, led by Senior Engineer, Michael Kim, has been working on optimizing this stage to reduce the time by 30% over the next quarter. The test stage is where the automated tests are run, which includes unit tests, integration tests, and UI tests. We use a combination of JUnit and Selenium for our testing framework, which was chosen by our QA team, headed by Emily Chen. The deploy stage is where the artifact is deployed to the production environment. We use Kubernetes to manage our containerized applications, which has been in place since February 2020. Our team has seen a significant reduction in deployment time, from 2 hours to just 15 minutes, after implementing Kubernetes. The pipeline is triggered by a Git push event, which is monitored by our Git repository manager, GitLab. The entire pipeline process is monitored by our DevOps team, who are responsible for ensuring that the pipeline is running smoothly and that any issues are quickly resolved. The team uses a dashboard to monitor the pipeline, which is updated in real-time. The dashboard is built using Grafana, which was chosen for its ease of use and customization options. In addition to the dashboard, the team also receives email notifications when any stage of the pipeline fails. The notification system was set up by our IT department, led by David Patel, who has over 5 years of experience in IT management. The pipeline has been running smoothly for the past year, with an average success rate of 95%. The team is constantly working to improve the pipeline, with a goal of reaching a 99% success rate by the end of the year. To achieve this goal, the team will be implementing new automated tests and optimizing the build stage. The optimized build stage is expected to reduce the overall pipeline time by 25%, which will have a significant impact on our development team's productivity. With the CI/CD pipeline in place, our development team has been able to deliver high-quality software applications quickly and efficiently, which has resulted in increased customer satisfaction and revenue growth for NovaTech. As of March 2023, our customer satisfaction rating has increased by 20%, and our revenue has grown by 15%. The success of the pipeline has also led to an increase in developer productivity, with an average reduction in development time of 30%. The pipeline has been a key factor in our company's success, and we will continue to monitor and improve it to ensure that it remains a vital part of our development process. The future plans for the pipeline include implementing new features, such as automated code reviews and security scans, which will further improve the quality and security of our software applications.
