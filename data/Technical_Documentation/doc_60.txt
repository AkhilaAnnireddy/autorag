Infrastructure as code, or IaC, has become a crucial aspect of modern IT operations. According to a study by Gartner, the adoption of IaC tools has increased by 50% in the last two years, with 75% of organizations planning to implement IaC by 2025. At NovaTech, we have been using Terraform for our IaC needs since 2020, with our team led by Senior Engineer, Rachel Lee. The decision to use Terraform was made after a thorough evaluation of other tools, including AWS CloudFormation and Azure Resource Manager. One of the key factors that influenced our decision was the large community of users and the extensive library of modules available for Terraform. As of January 2023, our Terraform configuration consists of over 500 lines of code, spread across 10 different modules. Each module is responsible for provisioning a specific component of our infrastructure, such as our web servers or databases. We have also implemented a policy of regularly reviewing and updating our Terraform configuration to ensure it remains up-to-date and secure. This policy, known as the 'Terraform Review Policy', was introduced in June 2022 and has been instrumental in reducing the number of errors in our configuration. The policy requires that all changes to the configuration be reviewed by at least two members of the team before they are deployed to production. In addition to the Terraform Review Policy, we have also implemented a number of other policies and procedures to ensure the security and integrity of our infrastructure. These include the 'Infrastructure Change Management Policy' and the 'Disaster Recovery Policy', both of which were introduced in 2020. The Infrastructure Change Management Policy requires that all changes to the infrastructure be thoroughly tested and validated before they are deployed to production, while the Disaster Recovery Policy outlines the procedures to be followed in the event of a disaster or major outage. As part of our disaster recovery planning, we have implemented a number of measures to ensure business continuity, including regular backups of our data and the use of redundant systems. We have also established a number of key performance indicators, or KPIs, to measure the performance and reliability of our infrastructure. These KPIs include the uptime of our web servers, the response time of our databases, and the number of errors per hour. By tracking these KPIs, we are able to quickly identify and resolve any issues that may arise, ensuring that our infrastructure remains stable and secure. In the future, we plan to continue to expand and improve our infrastructure, with a focus on increasing scalability and reducing costs. As part of this effort, we are currently evaluating the use of Kubernetes for container orchestration, with a view to implementing it by the end of 2024. We are also exploring the use of other IaC tools, such as AWS Cloud Development Kit, to see if they can provide any benefits over our current Terraform implementation. By staying up-to-date with the latest developments in IaC and continuously evaluating and improving our infrastructure, we are able to ensure that our systems remain secure, efficient, and reliable, and that we are well-positioned to meet the needs of our business and our customers.
