
   Overtime and Compensation Policy
   Effective Date: January 1, 2022
   Last Updated: June 15, 2022

   Introduction:
   * The purpose of this policy is to provide a fair and consistent approach to overtime and compensation for all employees of XYZ Corporation.
   * This policy applies to all full-time and part-time employees, including managers, supervisors, and hourly workers.
   * The policy is designed to ensure compliance with all relevant laws and regulations, including the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA).

   Eligibility for Overtime:
   * The following employees are eligible for overtime pay:
     * All hourly employees who work more than 40 hours in a workweek
     * All salaried employees who are classified as non-exempt under the FLSA
   * The following employees are not eligible for overtime pay:
     * All salaried employees who are classified as exempt under the FLSA
     * All employees who are paid on a commission-only basis

   Overtime Pay Rates:
   * Hourly employees who work overtime will be paid at a rate of 1.5 times their regular hourly rate
   * Salaried employees who are eligible for overtime will be paid at a rate of 1.5 times their regular hourly rate, based on their annual salary divided by 2,080 hours
   * The maximum overtime pay rate for any employee is $50 per hour

   Approval for Overtime:
   * All overtime must be approved in advance by the employee's supervisor or manager
   * Overtime requests must be submitted in writing, using the Overtime Request Form (available on the company intranet)
   * The following factors will be considered when approving or denying overtime requests:
     * Business needs and priorities
     * Employee workload and availability
     * Budget constraints

   Compensation for Overtime:
   * Overtime pay will be paid on the next regular pay date following the pay period in which the overtime was worked
   * Overtime pay will be included in the employee's regular paycheck, along with their regular pay
   * The following examples illustrate how overtime pay will be calculated:
     * Example 1: An hourly employee works 45 hours in a workweek, at a rate of $20 per hour. The employee's overtime pay would be $30 (1.5 x $20 x 5 hours)
     * Example 2: A salaried employee works 50 hours in a workweek, at a rate of $40,000 per year. The employee's overtime pay would be $48 (1.5 x ($40,000 / 2,080) x 10 hours)

   Record-Keeping and Reporting:
   * All overtime worked must be recorded and reported accurately and promptly
   * Employees are responsible for recording their own overtime hours, using the company's time-keeping system
   * Supervisors and managers are responsible for reviewing and approving employee time records, to ensure accuracy and completeness

   Training and Education:
   * All employees will receive training and education on this policy, during their orientation and on an annual basis
   * The training will cover the following topics:
     * Eligibility for overtime
     * Overtime pay rates
     * Approval for overtime
     * Compensation for overtime
     * Record-keeping and reporting
