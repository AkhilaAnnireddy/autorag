
   Expense Reimbursement Policy

   Introduction:
   * The purpose of this policy is to provide guidelines for reimbursing employees for expenses incurred while conducting company business.
   * This policy applies to all employees of XYZ Corporation.
   * The policy is effective as of January 1, 2022, and will be reviewed and updated annually.

   Eligible Expenses:
   * The following expenses are eligible for reimbursement:
     * Transportation costs (airfare, train, rental car, etc.)
     * Meals and entertainment (up to $50 per day)
     * Lodging (up to $150 per night)
     * Business-related phone and internet expenses
   * The following expenses are not eligible for reimbursement:
     * Personal expenses (gifts, souvenirs, etc.)
     * Expenses related to personal travel or entertainment
     * Fines or penalties (parking tickets, etc.)

   Reimbursement Procedure:
   * Employees must submit expense reports within 30 days of incurring the expense.
   * Expense reports must include:
     * Date and location of the expense
     * Description of the expense
     * Amount of the expense
     * Receipts or other supporting documentation
   * Reimbursements will be made on a bi-weekly basis.
   * The reimbursement process is managed by the Accounting Department, led by Jane Smith, who can be reached at (555) 123-4567 or jane.smith@xyzcorp.com.

   Approval Authority:
   * The following managers have approval authority for expense reports:
     * Department managers for expenses up to $500
     * Division managers for expenses between $501 and $2,000
     * Vice presidents for expenses over $2,000
   * Approval must be obtained prior to incurring the expense, whenever possible.
   * In exceptional cases, approval may be obtained after the expense has been incurred, but this must be justified and approved by the relevant manager.

   Record Keeping:
   * All expense reports and supporting documentation must be retained for a minimum of 3 years.
   * Records must be kept in a secure and confidential manner.
   * The Accounting Department is responsible for ensuring that all records are properly maintained and retained.

   Auditing and Compliance:
   * The company reserves the right to audit expense reports and supporting documentation at any time.
   * Employees are expected to comply with all company policies and procedures related to expense reimbursement.
   * Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

   Examples of Reimbursable Expenses:
   * Airfare: $500 for a round-trip ticket from New York to Los Angeles
   * Lodging: $120 per night for a hotel stay in Chicago
   * Meals and entertainment: $40 for dinner at a restaurant in San Francisco
   * Business-related phone and internet expenses: $100 per month for a phone and internet plan

   Expense Report Form:
   * The company provides an expense report form that must be used for all expense reports.
   * The form can be obtained from the Accounting Department or downloaded from the company intranet.
   * The form must be completed in full and submitted with all supporting documentation.

   Changes to the Policy:
   * This policy may be amended or updated at any time, with or without notice.
   * Changes to the policy will be effective immediately, unless otherwise specified.
   * Employees are responsible for familiarizing themselves with the current policy and any changes that may be made.

   Contact Information:
   * For questions or concerns about this policy, please contact the Accounting Department at (555) 123-4567 or accounting@xyzcorp.com.
   * The policy is also available on the company intranet, under the 'HR Policies' section.

   As of the last review on December 15, 2023, this policy remains in effect. It is the responsibility of each employee to adhere to the guidelines outlined in this document. Any questions or concerns should be directed to the Accounting Department. The company will review and update this policy annually, or as needed, to ensure compliance with changing regulations and company needs.
