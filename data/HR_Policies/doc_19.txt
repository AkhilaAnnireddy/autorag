Conflict of Interest Policy
Introduction
The purpose of this policy is to ensure that all employees of XYZ Corporation are aware of their responsibilities to avoid conflicts of interest. A conflict of interest arises when an employee's personal interests could influence their decisions or actions in the performance of their job. As of January 1, 2022, all employees are required to disclose any potential conflicts of interest to their supervisor or HR representative.

Table 1: Disclosure Requirements
| Category | Description | Disclosure Requirement |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Financial Interests | Ownership or investment in a company that does business with XYZ Corporation | Yes |
| Personal Relationships | Family members or close friends who work for a company that does business with XYZ Corporation | Yes |
| Outside Employment | Employment or consulting work outside of XYZ Corporation | Yes |
| Gifts and Entertainment | Receipt of gifts or entertainment from a company that does business with XYZ Corporation | Yes |

Table 2: Examples of Conflicts of Interest
| Scenario | Conflict of Interest |
| --- | --- |
| An employee owns stock in a company that competes with XYZ Corporation | Yes |
| An employee's family member works for a company that supplies goods to XYZ Corporation | Yes |
| An employee accepts a gift from a company that is seeking to do business with XYZ Corporation | Yes |

Reporting Requirements
All employees are required to report any potential conflicts of interest to their supervisor or HR representative within 30 days of the occurrence. The report should include the nature of the conflict, the potential impact on the employee's job, and any proposed solutions. As of June 30, 2022, a total of 25 employees have reported potential conflicts of interest, with 15 being resolved and 10 still under review.

Table 3: Reporting Statistics
| Year | Number of Reports | Number of Resolved Reports |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 2020 | 10 | 5 |
| 2021 | 15 | 10 |
| 2022 | 25 | 15 |

Consequences of Non-Compliance
 Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. As stated by our CEO, John Smith, 'It is the responsibility of every employee to uphold the highest standards of integrity and to avoid any actions that could be perceived as a conflict of interest.'

Table 4: Disciplinary Actions
| Infraction | Disciplinary Action |
| --- | --- |
| First offense | Warning and mandatory training |
| Second offense | Suspension without pay |
| Third offense | Termination of employment |

Conclusion
In conclusion, it is the responsibility of every employee to be aware of and to comply with this conflict of interest policy. If you have any questions or concerns, please do not hesitate to contact your supervisor or HR representative.
