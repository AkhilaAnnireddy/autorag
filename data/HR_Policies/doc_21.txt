
      # Introduction to Parental Leave Policy
      The parental leave policy at XYZ Corporation is designed to provide employees with a supportive and flexible work environment, allowing them to balance their work and family responsibilities. This policy applies to all regular full-time and part-time employees who have completed at least six months of service with the company.

      ## Eligibility Criteria
      To be eligible for parental leave, employees must have completed their probationary period and have a minimum of six months of continuous service with XYZ Corporation. The leave is available to both biological and adoptive parents, as well as employees who become legal guardians of a child.

      ### Leave Entitlement
      Eligible employees are entitled to up to 12 weeks of paid parental leave, which can be taken within the first year of the child's birth or adoption. The leave can be taken in a continuous block or split into two separate periods, with the approval of the employee's supervisor and the HR department.

      # Leave Application Procedure
      Employees who wish to take parental leave must submit a leave application form to the HR department at least 30 days prior to the expected start date of the leave. The application form must include the expected start and end dates of the leave, as well as a birth certificate or adoption papers for the child.

      ## Leave Payment
      During the parental leave period, employees will receive 100% of their base salary, up to a maximum of $5,000 per week. The leave payment will be made bi-weekly, on the regular pay dates.

      ### Benefits and Job Security
      Employees on parental leave are entitled to continue their benefits, including health, dental, and vision insurance, as well as their 401(k) plan. The company will also continue to pay its portion of the benefits premiums during the leave period. Upon return to work, employees are guaranteed their previous job or a similar position with the same pay and benefits.

      # Return to Work
      Employees who take parental leave are expected to return to work at the end of the leave period, unless they have made prior arrangements with their supervisor and the HR department. Employees who do not return to work after the leave period may be required to repay the leave payments they received.

      ## Leave Extension
      In some cases, employees may be eligible for an extension of their parental leave, up to a maximum of 26 weeks. This extension is subject to approval by the employee's supervisor and the HR department, and must be supported by a doctor's note or other documentation.

      ### Leave and Vacation Time
      Employees who take parental leave are not required to use their vacation time during the leave period. However, they may choose to use their vacation time to extend their leave, with the approval of their supervisor and the HR department.

      # Employee Responsibilities
      Employees who take parental leave are responsible for ensuring that their work is covered during their absence, and for making arrangements for their return to work. They must also provide the HR department with regular updates on their leave status and expected return to work date.

      ## HR Department Responsibilities

The HR department must also maintain accurate records of employee leave, and ensure that employees are paid correctly during their leave period.
