Our company, GreenTech Inc., is committed to maintaining the highest standards of integrity and ethics in all aspects of our business. As stated by our CEO, Rachel Lee, 'we strive to create a work environment where employees feel empowered to report any concerns or wrongdoing without fear of retaliation.' To achieve this, we have established a comprehensive whistleblower policy, which is outlined below. 
   
   The purpose of this policy is to provide a framework for employees to report any suspected unethical or illegal behavior, while also ensuring the confidentiality and protection of the whistleblower. This policy applies to all employees, officers, and directors of GreenTech Inc. and its subsidiaries. 
   
   According to Section 3, Article 7 of our company's bylaws, 'all employees have a responsibility to report any wrongdoing or unethical behavior to their supervisor or the HR department.' In 2020, we received a total of 25 reports, with 15 being related to financial irregularities and 10 related to workplace harassment. 
   
   The following are some key points to note about our whistleblower policy: 
   - All reports will be treated confidentially and investigated promptly.
   - Employees who make a report in good faith will be protected from retaliation.
   - Reports can be made anonymously, but this may limit our ability to investigate the matter fully.
   - The HR department will keep a record of all reports and their outcomes.
   
   We have a dedicated whistleblower hotline, which can be reached at 1-800-GREEN-01. This hotline is available 24/7 and is operated by an independent third-party provider. In 2022, we received a total of 30 reports through this hotline, with 20 being related to financial irregularities and 10 related to workplace harassment. 
   
   The investigation process will typically involve the following steps: 
   1. Initial review: The HR department will review the report to determine whether it requires further investigation.
   2. Investigation: A thorough investigation will be conducted, which may involve interviewing witnesses and gathering evidence.
   3. Report to management: The findings of the investigation will be reported to management, who will determine the appropriate course of action.
   4. Follow-up: The HR department will follow up with the whistleblower to provide an update on the outcome of the investigation.
