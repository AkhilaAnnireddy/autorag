Our company, NovaTech Inc., is committed to maintaining a professional and respectful work environment. As such, we have established a dress code policy to ensure that all employees present themselves in a manner that is consistent with our company values.

The purpose of this policy is to:
* Promote a professional and respectful image of the company
* Ensure that all employees are aware of the expected standards of dress and grooming
* Provide a safe and healthy work environment

The scope of this policy applies to all NovaTech Inc. employees, including full-time, part-time, and contract workers. This policy will be effective as of January 1, 2024, and will be reviewed and updated annually by the HR department.

Responsibilities:
* All employees are expected to adhere to the dress code policy and to report any concerns or incidents to their supervisor or HR representative
* Supervisors and managers are responsible for ensuring that their team members are aware of and comply with the dress code policy
* The HR department is responsible for developing, implementing, and enforcing the dress code policy

Dress Code Guidelines:
* Business attire is required for all client-facing employees, including:
    + Suits and ties for men
    + Professional dresses or pantsuits for women
* Business casual attire is allowed for non-client facing employees, including:
    + Dress pants or jeans
    + Blouses or casual shirts
* The following items are not permitted in the workplace:
    + Torn or frayed clothing
    + Clothing with offensive language or images
    + Open-toed shoes or sandals
* The following items are permitted in the workplace, but must be worn in a manner that is consistent with the company's professional image:
    + Jewelry, such as earrings, necklaces, and rings
    + Hair accessories, such as hats, scarves, and headbands

Exceptions to the dress code policy may be made for certain events or activities, such as:
* Company-sponsored events, such as holiday parties or team-building activities
* Client events, such as conferences or meetings
* Special projects or assignments that require a specific dress code

In these cases, employees will be notified in advance of the exception and will be expected to adhere to the specified dress code.

Enforcement of the dress code policy will be the responsibility of the HR department, in conjunction with supervisors and managers. Employees who fail to comply with the dress code policy may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including a written warning or termination.

As of 2022, our company has implemented a dress code committee, which consists of 5 members, including John Smith, the CEO, and 4 other representatives from different departments. The committee meets quarterly to review and update the dress code policy.

In 2023, we have received a total of 20 complaints regarding the dress code policy, and we have taken the necessary actions to address these concerns. We have also provided training to all employees on the dress code policy, which includes a 30-minute presentation and a 10-question quiz.

The dress code policy will be reviewed and updated annually, and all changes will be communicated to employees through the company's intranet and email. The policy will also be available in print form at the HR department.

We believe that our dress code policy is an important part of maintaining a professional and respectful work environment, and we expect all employees to adhere to it. If you have any questions or concerns about the dress code policy, please do not hesitate to reach out to the HR department.

* Can I make exceptions to the dress code policy?
* What is the process for reporting concerns or incidents related to the dress code policy?
