# Introduction to Workplace Safety Policy
The purpose of this Workplace Safety Policy is to provide a safe and healthy work environment for all employees, contractors, and visitors at Smithson Corporation. This policy outlines the responsibilities and procedures for maintaining a safe workplace and ensuring compliance with relevant laws and regulations.
## Policy Statement
Smithson Corporation is committed to providing a safe and healthy work environment for all employees, contractors, and visitors. We believe that all work-related injuries and illnesses are preventable, and we are committed to achieving a zero-harm workplace.
## Scope
This policy applies to all Smithson Corporation employees, contractors, and visitors at all company locations, including but not limited to offices, warehouses, and construction sites.
## Responsibilities
### Employee Responsibilities
All employees are responsible for:
* Following all safety procedures and guidelines
* Reporting any hazards or incidents to their supervisor or the Safety Officer
* Participating in safety training and drills
### Supervisor Responsibilities
All supervisors are responsible for:
* Ensuring that all employees under their supervision are aware of and follow all safety procedures and guidelines
* Conducting regular safety inspections and reporting any hazards or incidents to the Safety Officer
* Providing safety training and drills to employees under their supervision
## Safety Procedures
### Emergency Procedures
In the event of an emergency, all employees are required to follow the emergency procedures outlined below:
1. Call 911 or the local emergency number
2. Activate the fire alarm or other warning systems as necessary
3. Evacuate the building or area immediately
4. Assemble at the designated assembly point
### First Aid and Medical Response
Smithson Corporation provides first aid and medical response services to all employees. In the event of a medical emergency, employees should:
1. Call 911 or the local emergency number
2. Contact the Safety Officer or their supervisor
3. Provide basic first aid if trained to do so
## Hazard Reporting and Incident Investigation
### Hazard Reporting
All employees are encouraged to report any hazards or potential hazards to their supervisor or the Safety Officer. Hazards can be reported using the Hazard Reporting Form, which is available on the company intranet.
### Incident Investigation
In the event of a work-related injury or illness, an incident investigation will be conducted to determine the root cause and implement corrective actions. The incident investigation will be conducted by the Safety Officer and/or other designated personnel.
## Safety Training and Drills
### Safety Training
All employees are required to participate in safety training, which includes but is not limited to:
* New employee orientation
* Job-specific safety training
* Annual safety refresher training
### Safety Drills
Smithson Corporation conducts regular safety drills to ensure that all employees are prepared in the event of an emergency. Safety drills include but are not limited to:
* Fire drills
* Earthquake drills
* Emergency evacuation drills
## Record Keeping and Compliance
### Record Keeping
Smithson Corporation maintains accurate and detailed records of all safety-related activities, including but not limited to:
* Safety training records
* Incident investigation records
* Hazard reporting records
### Compliance
Smithson Corporation is committed to complying with all relevant laws and regulations, including but not limited to:
* Occupational Safety and Health Act (OSHA)
* Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)
* Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) regulations
## Review and Revision
This policy will be reviewed and revised as necessary, but at least annually. The Safety Officer is responsible for reviewing and revising this policy and ensuring that all employees are aware of any changes.
