
   Working Capital Report for XYZ Corporation as of December 31, 2022

   Introduction:
   - The purpose of this report is to analyze the working capital of XYZ Corporation.
   - Working capital is the difference between a company's current assets and current liabilities.
   - It is a crucial metric for assessing a company's liquidity and ability to meet its short-term obligations.

   Current Assets:
   * Cash and cash equivalents: $1,000,000
   * Accounts receivable: $2,500,000
   * Inventory: $3,000,000
   * Total current assets: $6,500,000

   Current Liabilities:
   * Accounts payable: $1,200,000
   * Short-term debt: $1,500,000
   * Total current liabilities: $2,700,000

   Working Capital Calculation:
   - Working capital = Total current assets - Total current liabilities
   - Working capital = $6,500,000 - $2,700,000
   - Working capital = $3,800,000

   Analysis:
   - The working capital of XYZ Corporation as of December 31, 2022, is $3,800,000.
   - This indicates that the company has sufficient liquidity to meet its short-term obligations.
   - The company's current ratio is 2.41 ($6,500,000 / $2,700,000), which is considered healthy.

   Recommendations:
   * The company should maintain its current level of working capital to ensure continued liquidity.
   * The company should consider investing in new inventory management systems to improve efficiency.
   * The company should review its accounts payable and accounts receivable policies to optimize cash flow.

   Conclusion:
   - The working capital report for XYZ Corporation as of December 31, 2022, indicates a healthy liquidity position.
   - The company's working capital is $3,800,000, which is sufficient to meet its short-term obligations.
   - The company should continue to monitor its working capital and make adjustments as necessary to maintain its liquidity.
