This Indemnification Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into on February 10, 2023, by and between GreenTech Inc., a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business at 123 Main Street, New York, NY 10001 ("GreenTech"), and Emily J. Miller, residing at 456 Elm Street, San Francisco, CA 94105 ("Miller"). GreenTech and Miller are collectively referred to herein as the "Parties" and individually as a "Party". The Parties are entering into this Agreement in connection with Miller's service as a member of the Board of Directors of GreenTech (the "Board"). GreenTech desires to indemnify Miller to the fullest extent permitted by law and GreenTech's Bylaws, and Miller is willing to serve as a director and officer of GreenTech on the condition that she is indemnified as set forth in this Agreement. As of the date of this Agreement, GreenTech's Bylaws (the "Bylaws") provide that GreenTech shall indemnify its directors and officers to the fullest extent permitted by law. On January 1, 2022, GreenTech's Board approved a policy for indemnification of directors and officers, which is attached hereto as Exhibit A (the "Indemnification Policy"). The Indemnification Policy provides for advancement of expenses and indemnification to the fullest extent permitted under Delaware law. As consideration for Miller's agreement to serve as a director, GreenTech agrees to indemnify Miller against all losses, claims, damages, and expenses (including attorneys' fees) that she may incur as a result of her service as a director, subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement. The term "losses" as used in this Agreement shall include, without limitation, any judgment, award, settlement, or other monetary liability, as well as any excise tax or penalty, imposed on or incurred by Miller in connection with any proceeding. GreenTech shall indemnify Miller to the fullest extent permitted by law against all losses that she may incur by reason of the fact that she is or was a director of GreenTech. The indemnification provided by this Section 2 shall be in addition to any other rights to which Miller may be entitled under GreenTech's Certificate of Incorporation, the Bylaws, any other agreement, or any vote of stockholders or disinterested directors. Notwithstanding the foregoing, GreenTech shall not be obligated to indemnify Miller for any losses incurred by reason of her gross negligence or willful misconduct. As used in this Agreement, the term "proceeding" means any threatened, pending, or completed action, suit, or proceeding, whether civil, criminal, administrative, or investigative. On August 15, 2022, GreenTech entered into a similar indemnification agreement with another director, David L. Brown. GreenTech has also purchased a directors' and officers' liability insurance policy (the "D&O Policy") from XYZ Insurance Company, which provides coverage for losses incurred by GreenTech's directors and officers, including Miller. The D&O Policy has a coverage limit of $10 million and a deductible of $250,000. The term of this Agreement shall commence on the date first above written and shall continue until Miller ceases to be a director of GreenTech. Upon the expiration or termination of this Agreement, GreenTech shall continue to indemnify Miller to the fullest extent permitted by law for any losses incurred by reason of the fact that she was a director of GreenTech during the term of this Agreement. In the event of any change in the Delaware General Corporation Law or other applicable law that narrows the circumstances under which indemnification is permissible, the obligations of GreenTech under this Agreement shall be automatically modified to the extent necessary to comply with such law. GreenTech shall give Miller prompt notice of any such change. The indemnification provided by this Agreement is intended to be as broad as permitted by the Delaware General Corporation Law, as amended from time to time. Any notice, request, or other communication required or permitted to be given under this Agreement shall be in writing and shall be deemed to have been duly given if delivered personally or sent by certified mail, return receipt requested, to the Party to whom it is directed at the address set forth above or at such other address as such Party shall have designated in writing. This Agreement may not be amended or modified except in a writing signed by both Parties. This Agreement shall be binding upon and inure to the benefit of the Parties and their respective successors, assigns, heirs, and personal representatives. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior agreements and understandings, both written and oral. By signing below, the Parties acknowledge that they have read, understand, and agree to be bound by the terms and conditions of this Agreement. The Parties agree that the effective date of this Agreement shall be February 10, 2023, and that it shall remain in effect until Miller ceases to be a director of GreenTech. On January 20, 2023, GreenTech's Board approved an increase in the coverage limit of the D&O Policy to $15 million, effective as of February 1, 2023. As of the date of this Agreement, the total amount of losses incurred by GreenTech's directors and officers under the D&O Policy is $500,000. GreenTech has also established a separate fund to cover any losses that may exceed the coverage limit of the D&O Policy. The fund has a balance of $2 million as of the date of this Agreement. The Indemnification Policy provides that GreenTech shall indemnify its directors and officers against all losses that they may incur as a result of their service to GreenTech, subject to certain limitations and exceptions. The Indemnification Policy also provides that GreenTech shall advance expenses to its directors and officers in connection with any proceeding, subject to certain conditions and limitations. On November 1, 2022, GreenTech entered into a settlement agreement with a former employee, which provided for the payment of $200,000 in exchange for the release of all claims against GreenTech and its directors and officers. The settlement agreement was approved by GreenTech's Board on October 25, 2022.
