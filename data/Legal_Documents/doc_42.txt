This Shareholder Agreement ('Agreement') is made and entered into on February 10, 2022, by and between GreenTech Inc., a corporation organized under the laws of the State of Delaware ('Company'), and its shareholders, namely, Emily J. Lee, Michael T. Brown, and David K. White ('Shareholders'). The Company was founded in 2018 with the primary objective of developing and commercializing innovative renewable energy solutions. As of the date of this Agreement, the Company's authorized capital stock consists of 10,000,000 shares of common stock, par value $0.001 per share. The Shareholders collectively own 100% of the Company's outstanding common stock, with Emily J. Lee owning 40%, Michael T. Brown owning 30%, and David K. White owning 30%. The parties hereto acknowledge that this Agreement supersedes all prior negotiations, understandings, and agreements between the parties regarding the subject matter hereof. The following terms and conditions shall govern the relationship between the Company and its Shareholders. The Company's board of directors shall consist of three members, with each Shareholder being entitled to appoint one director. The board of directors shall have the power to manage the business and affairs of the Company, and all major decisions shall require the approval of at least two-thirds of the board members. The Company's fiscal year shall commence on January 1 and end on December 31 of each year. The Company shall maintain accurate and complete records of its business and affairs, including but not limited to financial statements, meeting minutes, and shareholder records. The Shareholders agree to indemnify and hold harmless the Company and its officers, directors, employees, and agents against any and all claims, damages, and expenses arising out of or related to their actions or omissions as Shareholders. In the event of a dispute between the parties, the dispute shall be resolved through arbitration in accordance with the rules of the American Arbitration Association. The Company's transfer agent shall be responsible for maintaining the Company's stock ledger and processing all stock transfers. The transfer agent shall be appointed by the board of directors and shall serve for a term of two years. The following are the key policies and procedures that the Company shall adhere to: 

* The Company shall maintain a minimum cash balance of $100,000 at all times. 
* The Company shall not incur any debt or liability without the prior approval of the board of directors. 
* The Company shall maintain adequate insurance coverage for its business and assets. 
* The Company shall comply with all applicable laws and regulations. 

The Company's management team consists of the following individuals: 

1. Emily J. Lee - Chief Executive Officer 
2. Michael T. Brown - Chief Financial Officer 
3. David K. White - Chief Operating Officer 

Their responsibilities and duties are as follows: 

| Name | Title | Responsibilities | 
| --- | --- | --- | 
| Emily J. Lee | CEO | Overall strategy and direction | 
| Michael T. Brown | CFO | Financial management and planning | 
| David K. White | COO | Operations and management | 

The Shareholders have agreed to the following valuation methodology for the Company: 

* The Company's valuation shall be determined based on its financial performance, including revenue and earnings. 
* The valuation shall be reviewed and updated annually by the board of directors. 
* The valuation shall be used to determine the value of the Shareholders' ownership interests in the Company. 

The Company has established the following committees to assist the board of directors in its decision-making: 

1. Audit Committee: responsible for overseeing the Company's financial reporting and auditing processes. 
2. Compensation Committee: responsible for determining the compensation and benefits of the Company's executive officers. 
3. Nominating and Governance Committee: responsible for identifying and nominating candidates for election to the board of directors. 

The Shareholders have agreed to the following restrictions on the transfer of their shares: 

* The Shareholders shall not transfer any of their shares without the prior approval of the board of directors. 
* The Shareholders shall not transfer any of their shares to a competitor of the Company. 
* The Shareholders shall not transfer any of their shares to a person or entity that is not a citizen or resident of the United States.

The Company's bylaws shall be amended from time to time as necessary to reflect changes in the Company's business and affairs. The bylaws shall be adopted by the board of directors and shall be effective immediately upon adoption. The Company shall provide each Shareholder with a copy of the bylaws and any amendments thereto. The Shareholders acknowledge that they have read and understood the bylaws and shall comply with them at all times. 

The Company shall hold an annual meeting of its Shareholders on the first Monday in May of each year, at which meeting the Shareholders shall elect directors, consider and act upon any other matters that may be properly brought before the meeting. The annual meeting shall be held at the Company's principal executive office, unless otherwise determined by the board of directors. The Company shall provide each Shareholder with written notice of the annual meeting at least 30 days prior to the date of the meeting. The notice shall include the date, time, and place of the meeting, as well as a description of the matters to be considered and acted upon at the meeting. 

In the event of a deadlock between the Shareholders, the following procedures shall be followed: 

1. The Shareholders shall attempt to resolve the deadlock through negotiation and mediation. 
2. If the deadlock is not resolved through negotiation and mediation, the Shareholders shall submit the matter to arbitration in accordance with the rules of the American Arbitration Association. 
3. The arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding on the Shareholders. 

The Company shall maintain the confidentiality of its business and affairs, including but not limited to its financial statements, meeting minutes, and shareholder records. The Shareholders shall not disclose any confidential information to any person or entity without the prior written consent of the Company. 

The Company shall comply with all applicable laws and regulations, including but not limited to the Securities Act of 1933 and the Securities Exchange Act of 1934. The Company shall file all required reports and statements with the Securities and Exchange Commission, including but not limited to its annual report on Form 10-K and its quarterly reports on Form 10-Q. 

The Company shall provide each Shareholder with a copy of its annual report on Form 10-K and its quarterly reports on Form 10-Q, as filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission. The reports shall be provided to the Shareholders within 30 days after filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission. 

The Shareholders acknowledge that they have read and understood this Agreement and shall comply with it at all times. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior negotiations, understandings, and agreements between the parties regarding the subject matter hereof.
